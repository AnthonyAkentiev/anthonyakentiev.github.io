More (Non-technical) Books

| Comments

Hi. I’m happy to introduce you my latest article on non-technical books that i’ve read. I hope that it will help you to select some cool reads.

Business Adventures: Twelve Classic Tales from the World of Wall Street (John Brooks)

here

“One of my favourite business books” (by Bill Gates).

This book was written in 60th. It contains many business stories about Ford, GM, IBM, etc. 60th was a very remarkable time. I was really shocked by reading how USA citizens started using Xerox copier machines back in 1963. This side of the book is very interesting for non-USA citizens like me.

However - i’d give 2/5 stars only. The book is little boring and not as interesting as Bill Gates once stated.

Our Kids: The American Dream in Crisis (Robert D. Putnam)

here

Do you want to live in USA? Check this book first (he-he). It is about growing gap between wealthy and poor people in USA. The problem is getting worst every year. USA became home for inequality, completely opposite to what USA always declarated as a basic principle - “country of liberty and equal rights”.

Main line of this book is “1950 vs 2000+” comparison. I highly recommend you this book because of rather gloomy view of USA of today: drugs, violence, bad schools, separation, racism. 3/5 stars.

The Long Tail: How Endless Choice is Creating Unlimited Demand (Chris Anderson)

here

The world of today is where everything (music, books, video, knowledge…) is available to everyone at a click of mouse. Half of music tracks that we buy today are made by completely “unknown” artists.

Scroll Amazon/iTunes list to the end - and you will see that even the least downloaded tracks are still downloaded couple of times. “Mainstream”, “boys bands”, “blockbusters”, “hits” are almost dead words. If you want some rare Japanese noise music - you can get it.

How this changed people, markets and demand? The book will provide you with the answer.

The theory is that mass culture is fading, and being replaced by a series of niches

I’m often asked about the effect of Long Tail on pricing. Should prices go down with demand as you travel down the Trail? Or should they rise, as more specific and narrowly focused goods appeal more strongly to their niche audiences?

Another sort of “hit” is major cities

We’ve changed from “i want to be normal” to “i want to be special”

The top 10 words account only for 3% of all searches

Is a fragmented culture a better or worse culture? Many believe that mass culture serves as a sort of social glue, keeping society together. But if we’re now all off doing our own thing, is there still a common culture? Are our interests stil aligned with those of our neighbors?

4/5 stars.

p.s. There is one interesting article about “death of mainstream” (in Russian) - see it here.

Thinking, Fast and Slow (Daniel Kahneman)

here

“Kahneman provides a detailed, yet accessible, description of the psychological mechanisms involved in making decisions”.

Humans have 2 systems - based on intuition - “fast” (wow, this car is very expensive) and based on logics - “slow” (this car is much cheaper in the long run). Want to know how these systems work? Don’t know what “behavioural economics” is?

Read this awesome book even if you are not related to marketing or sales.

There are few circumstances under which it is a good ideat to substitute judgment for a formula

Remember this rule: intuition cannot be trusted in the absence of stable regularities in the environment

This is the case of overconfidence. They seem to believe they know more than they really do know

5/5 stars. Highly recommended.

Nudge: Improving Decisions About Health, Wealth, and Happiness (Richard H. Thaler)

here

One more book on marketing and “behavioural economics”. It is great to provide people with “default options” and allow them to “opt out”. Marketer always wanted to tell people what they want to buy. “Nudge” is not a raw manipulation, but a little “push” to one side.

Want organic food? Need some Light beer? The best color for Honda is pearl white.

The book is repetitive and boring. Better read some shorter articles on that topic. If you already read “Thinking, Fast and Slow” book (see above) - “Nudge” will be waste of time.

2/5 stars.

Rainmaking Conversations: Influence, Persuade, and Sell in Any Situation (Mike Schultz)

here

“In business, a rainmaker is a person who brings in new business and wins new accounts almost by magic, since it is often not readily apparent how this new business activity is caused”.

The book is about selling and marketing too (see books above): how to get right feedback, take actions, give people more value than before. It is easy to read and interesting for non-marketers.

4/5 stars.

Thinkertoys: A Handbook of Creative-Thinking Techniques (Michael Michalko)

here

Want to be creative? Don’t know how to solve your current business/techical problem? How to brainstorm idea? How to transform idea? How to pivot your startup?

Open your mind and buy a copy of this book. Now )

5/5 stars.

I Could Do Anything If I Only Knew What It Was (Barbara Sher)

here

Whom you want to be 5 years later? 10 year from now? Change your job? Be a musician or blogger? Don’t panic, everything is possible, if you will have a plan.

Highly motivational stuff here. I don’t need all of that (he-he), so i skipped almost half of this book. Can’t give it a score, because if is out of my scope.

SECRET BOOK (IN RUSSIAN) is HERE

here

Yeah! And now little about real RUSSIAN business (however, in Russian only). Bears, vodka, caviar and other funny Russian stuff included. Very entertaining and easy to read. C’mon, read it!
